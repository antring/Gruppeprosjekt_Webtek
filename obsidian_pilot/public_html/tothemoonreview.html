To the Moon

2014/04/07 - Vidar "VidyPie" Nedregård

If you one day got the chance to turn back time, and relive your life. The ability to change things, focus on other goals, maybe go a different direction in a important decision. Would you do it? Or would you let things remain as they are, and not try to change anything?

Such feels

It is over two years since I picked up To the Moon the first time. Voluntary it was not. I was almost forced by a friend to play through it, as it apparently was be the best game ever. Reluctantly I got right in front of the computer screen, and nothing could have prepared me for what was to come.

To report this little game is difficult, since I'm afraid to reveal too much. What I can say, and also show in the trailer, is that the game is about an old man named Johnny, and the setting is a bit into the future. Johnny is on his deathbed, and has contacted Sigmund Corp.. to fulfill one last wish. Sigmund Corp.. delivers a rather unique service. They can tap into the mind of a person, and plant small seeds in classical inception style that will change the persons memories of his life.

The Job assignment falls on Dr. Neil Watts and Dr. Eva Rosalene, and it is these two characters you switch between playing as. They arrive at the house of Johnny, set up the necessary equipment, and get ready to jump into hs mind and change his memories. The only problem is that the Johnyys desire is quite diffuse. He wants to travel to the moon but cannot remember why. Anyway, a job is a job, and Neil and Eva jump in with their heads held high.

The seeds that will change the memories of Johnny have to be planted as early as possible in his life, that is, in the oldest memories. The problem is that these memories can not be reached immediately. Neil and Eva have to work backwards, and jump from one memory to the next. This means that the story is told backwards, and it works brilliantly.

STOP!

I decide to stop saying anything more about the story here, so that I don't spoil the experience for others. It takes about five hours to complete the game, but much longer to digest the story being told. Maybe you now think the same as I thought, that this is impossible a game for me. I still strongly recommend you to give this game a try.

The graphics may be very simple, and the same can be said about the game mechanics, but it means nothing for this game. The entire focus is on the history, and when you got a story that is this good, everything else is forgotten immediately. In addition, the game has an incredible piano soundtrack, which can make the toughest alpha male sensitive and vulnerable.

Epical

Games like The Last of Us and The Walking Dead has been praised for their stories and emotional moments. To the Moon deserves a place on the same shelf as these heavyweights, and is further proof that games, like books and movies, is a wonderful medium to tell a story.
