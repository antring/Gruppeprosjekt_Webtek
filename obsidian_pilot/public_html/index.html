Indie Corner - Week 1: To the Moon

Indie Corner is a weekly column that focuses on smaller games created by independent developers. In the first installment of this column we wipe the dust off a not too new game, but a game deserves to be mentioned and played by everyone.

Vikings

History Channel has just given the green light for a third season of the historical drama Vikings. The series is currently in the middle of season 2, and every Friday I follow legendary Vikings raid while I eat mutton and drink brown beer. For those who have not given the series a chance yet, I recommend to read my review.



Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey

“Every person you’ve ever heard of lies right in there. All those kings and battles, migrations and inventions, wars and loves, every thing in the history books happened here in the last 14 seconds of the cosmic calendar.”And so it returns. Cosmos. Shunned by the Christian community and loved by childish, dreamy explorers all over the world. The documentary that unknowingly changed the course of countless human lives and became an instant scientific classic.

The Wolf of Wall Street

Meet Jordan Belfort. Self proclaimed «Wolf of Wall Street», High-rolling millionaire at 26, snorting coke, popping pills and slamming prostitutes on a daily basis. According to himself, that's' how you make it big on Wall Street. The way Martin Scorese portray the cynic millionaire is delightfully old-school (Remember GoodFellas), and makes poor Gordon Gekko look like a pup in comparison.


Goat Simulator

Clearly this is a worthy candidate for game of the year 2014. Since the year not yet has passed, the verdict have to wait. But if you want to have a laugh and most important of all. Lick a passing hangglider as a goat. This is the game for you! And of course, the game is exclusive to the PC gaming masterrace!



Only God Forgives

Only God Forgives is the latest film by Danish director/auteur Nicolas Winding Refn and it stars Ryan Gosling as the main character. Because of these two factors one could be quick to assume that it would be similar to the director's last film, Drive, also starring Ryan Gosling. The similarities are there for sure, but this film is its own beast, not relying on Drive's success with the general public. The film is significantly more artistic and ethereal, relying heavily on symbolism and hidden themes instead of a straightforward plot.

The Third Eye

The third eye is a Norwegian crime show, with Kyrre Haugen Sydness in the lead role as police detective Viggo Lust. Four years ago a nightmare for any parent happened to Viggo, his five-year-old daughter Christina vanished outside a store, when Viggo were inside buying ice cream for his daughter. There were no witnesses and nobody had seen anything, he were in the store for only a minute, and still his daughter vanished from here seat in the bicycle.
