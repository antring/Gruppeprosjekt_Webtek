The Wolf of Wall Street

2014/04/01 - Kristoffer Nesvik

(ObsidianPilot.com): Meet Jordan Belfort. Self proclaimed «Wolf of Wall Street», High-rolling millionaire at 26, snorting coke, popping pills and slamming prostitutes on a daily basis. According to himself, that's' how you make it big on Wall Street. The way Martin Scorese portray the cynic millionaire is delightfully old-school (Remember GoodFellas), and makes poor Gordon Gekko look like a pup in comparison.

The movie is based on the true story about the real Jordan Belfort (Portrayed by Leonardo DiCaprio), from his humble beginnings at L.F Rotschild; job description «smile and dial», to the rise and fall of his multi-million venture Stratton Oakmont.

Following the 1987 stock market crash leading to the fall of L.F Rotschild, Belfort joins a penny-stocks firm in Long Island, and with the help from a bunch of expert salesmen» (Mainly weed), including Mark Hanna (Matthew McConaughey) and Donnie Azoff (Jonah Hill), he sets out to form Stratton Oakmont.

Besides selling, Belfort and the gang knows another very important trade on Wall Street: partying. After blowing 16.000 dollars on a lunch, playing darts using midgets and snorting cocaine from the rear end of a prostitute, the face of law and order (Kyle Chandler) inevitably comes knocking on the door.

As the venture grows, he decides to marry the gorgeous model Naomi Lapaglia (Margot Robbie), buys an oversized yacht, a few homes and a helicopter (Said helicopter he later takes for a spin under the influence of a homemade Quaaludes, Xanax and morphine-coctail).

«I fucked her brains out.... for 11 seconds». Dark humor and complete honesty is what makes this tale of Belforts pursuit of happiness, money and glory such an outstanding movie.
For some, the movie is a tad too long, clocking in at exactly three hours, but it's never boring and manages to keep the viewers interest throughout.

Refreshingly different

The Wolf of Wall Street is refreshingly different compared to all the other Wall Street Hollywood productions springing up recently, and despite being a little rushed in post production, its a movie where DiCaprio, Jonah Hill and Margot Robbie really shines.
