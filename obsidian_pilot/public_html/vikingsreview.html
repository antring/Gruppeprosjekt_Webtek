Vikings

2014/04/01 - Vidar "VidyPie" Nedregård

The TV Series Vikings is History Channel's first attempt to produce its own historical drama. And it should be said that to be the first of its kind, it is invested heavily and ambitious with this series. Michael Hirst (The Tudors, Elizabeth) writes and produces the series, and on the cast we find strong actors like George Blagden (Les Misérables, Wrath of the Titans) and Sweden's big son Gustaf Skarsgård (The Way Back, Kon-Tiki, Evil). They are, however, placed in the shade of largely unknown Travis Fimmel, who plays the show's lead role.

Raiding in the west

Vikings takes place in the beginning of the Viking Age, around the start of the ninth century. The series' protagonist, Ragnar Lodbrok (Travis Fimmel), lives a simple life as a farmer with his wife Lagertha (Katheryn Winnick) and their two children, Bjorn (Nathan O'Toole) and Gyda (Ruby O'Leary). The family has sweared fealty to the local Earl, Earl Haraldsson (Gabriel Byrne), a simple man with small visions.

Every year the men of Earl Haraldson set out to plunder in the east. Those who are victims of the looting are usually poor farmers and fishermen, and raids end up with lean yield. Ragnar, however, has heard rumors of a country in the south with large riches. He has also found a way to navigate the ocean, and challenges Earl Haraldson to let the men go raid in the west.

Historical avalanche

As expected of a series produced by History Channel there has been placed great emphasis on historical realism. It has been taken som eliberty to increase the tension in the series, but I do not see this as negative.

Several of the characters in the series are based on real people from the Viking Age, or taken from the sagas and Norse Edda. Ragnar Lodbrok was a legendary Norwegian earl who is supposed to have been the greatest threat against England and France in the early 800s. In the series, he has a brother named Rollo (Clive Standen), based on the Viking Hrolf Ganger, which created a Viking colony in Normandy and besieged Paris. That these two were brothers in reality seems doubtful.

It is also focused heavily on that the way life in the Viking Age are portrayed is realistic and free of the usual clichés we often find in other films and TV series. You will not see many horned helmets. Extra positive is the way the highlights of the series, the battle scenes, are produced. Here we get a unique insight into the way the Vikings operated in combat, and what tactics they used. Battles is usually not a chaos where everyone is running around in a field trying to chop the head of another. They are however two defined armies standing shield against shield, poking each other, while they cry Odin and Valhalla.

«I’m not dead yet»

In season 1 the acting varied between absolute brilliant and downright awful. Thankfully the bad was forgotten quickly as soon as the blood began to float again on the screen. In season one the scenes with Floki(Skarsgard) were the highlights in terms of acting. In Season 2, however, Fimmel has been the strongest, and it would appear that Vikings will be the breakthrough for his acting career. Let's hope it continues in the same track, and that the producer is able to purge all the feigned Swedish and Danish dialects.

Lightweight

Vikings can not measure up to the epicness that is Game of Thrones. The Vikings however, is a series that is never boring or too lenghty. In a single episode Ragnar can cross the Norwegian sea several times, and it is constantly added new elements to push the story in new directions. Because of this Vikings is a series that is always enjoyable to watch, and each episode offers entertainment and the occasional bloodbath.
